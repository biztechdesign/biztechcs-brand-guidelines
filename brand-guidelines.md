## 1. Brand Overview

### Brand Name
**Biztech Consulting & Solutions** (abbreviated as **BiztechCS**)

### Brand Positioning
BiztechCS positions itself as a full-spectrum digital transformation partner for mid-market and enterprise businesses globally. With 19+ years of expertise, 1,300+ completed projects, and a 200+ member team, Biztech bridges the gap between business challenges and technology capabilities — delivering measurable outcomes rather than just code.

> *"Tech that works, so you don't have to."*

### Brand Personality

| Trait | Description |
|---|---|
| **Expert** | 19+ years of depth across product development, AI, ERP, and cloud |
| **Trustworthy** | 98% client retention; transparent, long-term partnerships |
| **Innovative** | Adoption of cutting-edge technologies including AI/ML and cloud-native |
| **Approachable** | Avoids jargon overload; speaks to business outcomes, not just tech |
| **Results-Driven** | Quantifies impact; uses numbers and metrics in messaging |
| **Growth-Oriented** | Positioned as a growth partner, not just a vendor |

### Tone and Voice

BiztechCS communicates with a **confident, consultative, and outcome-focused** voice. The brand is knowledgeable without being condescending, and ambitious without being hyperbolic.

- **Professional but human:** Not cold corporate-speak; real partnership language
- **Direct:** Leads with the benefit, then explains the how
- **Data-backed:** Reinforces claims with numbers (19+ years, 98% retention, 1300+ projects)
- **Action-oriented:** Every piece of content ends with a clear next step

### Target Audience

| Segment | Role | Need |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | CTOs, VPs of Engineering | Technical execution at scale, staff augmentation |
| **Primary** | CEOs, COOs of SMBs / Mid-market | Digital transformation, cost optimization |
| **Secondary** | Product Managers | MVP development, product iteration |
| **Secondary** | IT Procurement | Vendor evaluation, compliance, trust signals |
| **Tertiary** | Startup Founders | Rapid product development, MVP to scale |

**Geographies:** North America, Europe, Middle East, Australia, India

### Core Brand Attributes

1. **Reliability** — Long-standing client relationships, proven delivery track record
2. **Depth** — Expertise across 9+ technology stacks and 8+ industry verticals
3. **Partnership** — Growth-partner mindset, not transactional vendor
4. **Innovation** — Early adopter of AI, cloud-native, and modern frameworks
5. **Transparency** — Clear processes, milestone-based delivery, security-first

### Visual Style Summary

BiztechCS uses a **modern enterprise tech aesthetic**: clean white surfaces, deep navy/charcoal authority, and a vibrant yellow-orange accent that signals energy and forward momentum. The design language is minimal, grid-organized, and dense with proof points — statistics, logos, and case study cards that communicate scale and credibility. Illustrations and icons are custom, flat, and professional rather than generic stock visuals.

## 2. Logo Guidelines

### Logo Usage

The BiztechCS logo is the primary brand mark. It consists of a wordmark combining "Biztech" with a symbolic icon element. The logo should be used on all official communications, website properties, presentations, and marketing collateral.

**Primary logo:** Full color on white or light backgrounds
**Reversed logo:** White version on dark or colored backgrounds
**Monochrome logo:** Single-color (black or white) for embossed or single-color print applications

### Clear Space Recommendations

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the height of the capital letter "B" in the wordmark on all four sides of the logo. No other graphic elements, text, or visual noise should enter this protected zone.

```
[B height] = X

        X
    ┌──────────┐
 X  │  LOGO   │  X
    └──────────┘
        X
```

### Minimum Size Recommendations

| Application | Minimum Width |
|---|---|
| Digital (screen) | 120px |
| Print | 25mm |
| Favicon / App Icon | 32px (icon-only mark) |
| Email signature | 150px |

### Acceptable Logo Variations

1. **Full color horizontal** — Primary usage; wordmark + icon
2. **Full color stacked** — For square/compact placements (social avatars, app icons)
3. **White / reversed** — For dark backgrounds (footer, hero overlays, dark cards)
4. **Monochrome black** — Single-color print, emboss, deboss
5. **Icon only** — Favicon, app icon, small badge usage only

### Incorrect Logo Usage

- Do not stretch, skew, or distort the logo
- Do not recolor the logo outside approved palette variations
- Do not apply drop shadows or outer glows directly to the logo
- Do not place the full-color logo on a busy photographic background without sufficient contrast
- Do not recreate the logo using a different font
- Do not rotate the logo
- Do not enclose the logo in a shape (circle, square) unless it is the approved app-icon version
- Do not add taglines beneath the logo unless part of an approved lockup

### Placement Rules

- **Top-left** of headers and navigation bars — primary placement
- **Centered** in email headers, presentations, and certificate templates
- **Footer** — white/light reversed version on dark footer background
- Always use the correct contrast version for legibility

### Background Usage Recommendations

| Background Type | Recommended Logo Version |
|---|---|
| White / Light gray | Full color |
| Dark navy / Charcoal | White reversed |
| Yellow / Brand accent | Dark (charcoal or navy) |
| Photography | White reversed with semi-transparent dark scrim behind |
| Gradient (dark-to-light) | White or full color depending on end color |

## 5. Layout System

### 5.1 Container Widths

| Breakpoint | Container Max Width | Side Padding |
|---|---|---|
| Mobile (< 768px) | `100%` | `16px` |
| Tablet (768px–1023px) | `100%` | `24px` |
| Laptop (1024px–1279px) | `960px` | `32px` |
| Desktop (1280px–1535px) | `1200px` | `40px` |
| Wide (≥ 1536px) | `1440px` | `auto` |

### 5.2 Grid System

BiztechCS uses a **12-column grid** throughout:

| Context | Columns | Gutter |
|---|---|---|
| Hero layout | 12 (full) | `32px` |
| Service cards (desktop) | 3 or 4 per row | `24px` |
| Service cards (tablet) | 2 per row | `20px` |
| Service cards (mobile) | 1 per row | `16px` |
| Case study cards | 3 per row → 1 on mobile | `24px` |
| Blog cards | 3 per row → 1 on mobile | `24px` |
| Stats row | 4 per row → 2 on mobile | `24px` |
| Footer columns | 4–5 per row → 2 on mobile | `32px` |

### 5.3 Section Spacing

| Context | Top/Bottom Padding |
|---|---|
| Full-page sections (desktop) | `80px – 100px` |
| Full-page sections (tablet) | `60px` |
| Full-page sections (mobile) | `40px` |
| CTA banner sections | `60px – 80px` |
| Footer | `60px top / 24px bottom` |
| Hero sections | `100px – 120px` |

### 5.4 Breakpoints

```css
/* Breakpoint tokens */
--breakpoint-sm:  480px;   /* Large mobile */
--breakpoint-md:  768px;   /* Tablet portrait */
--breakpoint-lg: 1024px;   /* Laptop / tablet landscape */
--breakpoint-xl: 1280px;   /* Standard desktop */
--breakpoint-2xl: 1536px;  /* Wide/ultrawide */
```

### 5.5 Content Alignment Rules

- **Left-aligned:** Default for all body copy, card text, form labels
- **Center-aligned:** Section headings and eyebrow labels, stats rows, CTA sections, hero copy
- **Right-aligned:** Reserved for table cells (numeric data) and navigation utilities only
- **Full-width:** Hero sections, CTA banners, footer, partner logo strips

## 14. Interaction & Motion Guidelines

### 14.1 Core Principles

- **Purposeful:** Every animation communicates state or guides attention — no motion for decoration alone
- **Subtle:** Transforms are small (4px lift, not 20px), durations are short
- **Consistent:** Same easing and duration across equivalent interactions

### 14.2 Standard Timing

| Interaction type | Duration | Easing |
|---|---|---|
| Button hover | `200ms` | `ease` |
| Link color | `150ms` | `ease` |
| Card hover lift | `250ms` | `ease` |
| Dropdown open | `200ms` | `ease-out` |
| Modal open | `250ms` | `ease` |
| Accordion expand | `300ms` | `ease-in-out` |
| Page section scroll reveal | `600ms` | `cubic-bezier(0.16,1,0.3,1)` |
| Skeleton loading | `1500ms` | `ease-in-out` (pulsing) |
| Number counter (stats) | `2000ms` | `ease-out` |

### 14.3 Hover Animations

```css
/* Card lift */
.card { transition: transform 0.25s ease, box-shadow 0.25s ease; }
.card:hover { transform: translateY(-4px); box-shadow: var(--shadow-lg); }

/* Button scale (subtle) */
.btn:hover { transform: translateY(-1px); }
.btn:active { transform: translateY(0); }

/* Arrow slide (Explore More) */
.btn-text .arrow { transition: transform 0.2s ease; }
.btn-text:hover .arrow { transform: translateX(4px); }
```

### 14.4 Scroll Animations

- Sections fade up on enter: `opacity 0 → 1` + `translateY(24px → 0)`
- Duration: `600ms`, staggered `100ms` delay per item in a grid
- Use `IntersectionObserver` — trigger once only (not on scroll-up)
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`:

```css
@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) {
  *, *::before, *::after {
    animation-duration: 0.01ms !important;
    transition-duration: 0.01ms !important;
  }
}
```

### 14.5 Loading States

- Skeleton screens for card grids: pulsing gray bars matching content dimensions
- Spinner: `24px` circular, `#1A4F9C`, `1s linear` rotation
- Button loading: replace label with spinner (same button dimensions, no layout shift)

### 14.6 Micro-interactions

- Form input border animates to brand blue on focus (no flash)
- Checkbox/radio checked state: scale-in animation on the check mark
- Navigation dropdown: slides down `8px` + fades in
- Stat counters: count up from 0 on first viewport entry
- Toast notifications: slide in from top-right, auto-dismiss at `4s`

## 15. Accessibility Guidelines

### 15.1 Color Contrast

All text must meet **WCAG 2.1 AA** minimum:

| Text type | Minimum contrast ratio |
|---|---|
| Normal text (< 18px) | `4.5:1` |
| Large text (≥ 18px bold / ≥ 24px) | `3:1` |
| UI components, icons | `3:1` |

**Critical checks:**
- White text on `#1A4F9C` blue: ✓ passes AA
- `#1E2532` text on white: ✓ passes AAA
- `#F5A623` yellow on `#0B1F3A` navy: ✓ passes AA
- **Never use:** Yellow text on white background (fails contrast)

### 15.2 Keyboard Navigation

- All interactive elements must be reachable via `Tab` key in logical DOM order
- Skip-to-content link at page top (visually hidden until focused)
- Dropdown menus must be operable with arrow keys and closed with `Escape`
- Modal must trap focus within it when open; return focus to trigger on close
- Carousel/slider must support keyboard navigation

### 15.3 Focus States

Focus states must be visible and distinct — never use `outline: none` without a replacement:

```css
:focus-visible {
  outline: 3px solid #1A4F9C;
  outline-offset: 2px;
  border-radius: 2px;
}
/* For dark backgrounds */
.dark :focus-visible {
  outline-color: #F5A623;
}
```

### 15.4 Font Readability

- Minimum body font size: `14px` (never smaller for flowing copy)
- Line height minimum: `1.5` for body text
- Maximum line length: `70ch` for paragraph text
- Avoid all-caps for long text (short labels only)
- Never use font-weight below `400` for body text

### 15.5 Button & Link Accessibility

- All buttons must have a visible text label or `aria-label`
- Icon-only buttons require `aria-label` describing the action
- Links must make sense out of context (avoid "click here", "read more" as standalone)
- Distinguish links from non-link text visually beyond color alone (underline)

### 15.6 Form Accessibility

- Every input must have an associated `<label>` (not placeholder-only)
- Error messages must be announced by screen readers via `aria-live="polite"` or `aria-describedby`
- Required fields: `required` attribute + visual asterisk
- Field groupings: use `<fieldset>` + `<legend>` for radio/checkbox groups

### 15.7 Images

- All `<img>` elements must have `alt` text
- Decorative images use `alt=""` and optionally `role="presentation"`
- Complex infographics include a text description nearby or in the `alt` text

### 15.8 ARIA Usage

```html
<!-- Navigation -->
<nav aria-label="Main navigation">

<!-- Mobile menu toggle -->
<button aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="mobile-menu">

<!-- Accordion item -->
<button aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="panel-1">

<!-- Alert messages -->
<div role="alert" aria-live="polite">

<!-- Loading spinner -->
<div role="status" aria-label="Loading...">
```

## 16. Content Style Guide

### 16.1 Brand Voice

BiztechCS speaks like a **trusted senior technology partner** — confident, direct, and focused on business outcomes. The voice is warm but not casual, expert but not academic.

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Confident** | Own your expertise | "We deliver." not "We try to deliver." |
| **Outcome-first** | Lead with what the client gains | "Scale faster" not "We offer cloud services" |
| **Credible** | Back claims with data | "98% of clients stay — and here's why" |
| **Human** | Avoid cold corporate-speak | "Let's talk" not "Initiate a consultation" |
| **Precise** | Say it once, say it right | No padding, no filler sentences |

### 16.2 Tone Variation by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Homepage hero | Bold, visionary, inspiring |
| Service pages | Technical but accessible, problem → solution framing |
| Case studies | Factual, results-driven, storytelling arc |
| Blog posts | Educational, practical, thought-leadership |
| CTAs | Direct, action-oriented, benefit-clear |
| Error pages | Friendly, helpful, solution-offering |
| Forms | Reassuring, minimal friction |

### 16.3 Grammar & Style

- **Sentence case** for headings and subheadings (not Title Case for everything)
- **Title case** reserved for proper nouns and main H1/H2 page titles
- Use **Oxford comma** in lists
- Use **active voice** — "We built a platform" not "A platform was built by us"
- Numbers: spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10+
- Use "+" for stat callouts: "19+", "1,300+", not "more than 19"
- American English spelling: "optimize" not "optimise"
- Spell out acronyms on first use: "Customer Relationship Management (CRM)"

### 16.4 Headline Writing Rules

- Lead with the benefit or outcome, not the feature
- Use power words: "Scale", "Build", "Transform", "Deliver", "Accelerate"
- Avoid vague superlatives: "best", "world-class", "leading" (unless backed by data)
- Keep H1s under 10 words ideally
- Use a colon or em dash to add a second punch: "Built for Scale — Designed to Last"

### 16.5 CTA Wording

| Context | Preferred wording |
|---|---|
| Primary page CTA | "Talk to Experts", "Get Started", "Book a Free Call" |
| Service exploration | "Explore [Service Name]", "See How It Works" |
| Case study | "Read the Full Case Study" |
| Blog | "Read Article" |
| Form submit | "Request Consultation", "Send Message" |
| Hire section | "Hire [Role]", "Get a Quote" |

**Avoid:** "Submit", "Click here", "Learn More" (generic), "See More" without context

### 16.6 Do's and Don'ts for Copy

**Do:**
- Start with the reader's problem or goal
- Use short sentences for clarity
- Include specific numbers and proof points
- End sections with a clear next step

**Don't:**
- Use buzzword chains: "synergistic end-to-end agile solutions"
- Over-qualify: "potentially," "might possibly," "we believe we could"
- Start too many consecutive sentences with "We"
- Use exclamation marks in body copy (acceptable in friendly UI microcopy only)

## 18. Implementation Notes

### 18.1 How to Use the Brand System in HTML/CSS

1. **Import the token file first** — all colors, spacing, and typography reference CSS variables. Never hard-code a color value in a component stylesheet.

2. **Layer architecture (recommended):**
   ```
   tokens.css       → CSS variables (:root)
   base.css         → reset + base element styles (h1–h6, p, a, etc.)
   components/      → one file per component (card.css, button.css, nav.css)
   utilities.css    → spacing helpers, text utilities
   pages/           → page-specific overrides only
   ```

3. **Always use semantic color tokens**, not raw values:
   ```css
   /* ✅ Correct */
   color: var(--color-text-primary);
   background: var(--color-bg-dark);

   /* ❌ Incorrect */
   color: #1E2532;
   background: #0B1F3A;
   ```

### 18.2 Reusable Component Structure

Each component should follow this pattern:

```html
<!-- BEM naming: block__element--modifier -->
<div class="card card--service">
  <div class="card__icon">
    <img src="icon.svg" alt="" aria-hidden="true">
  </div>
  <h3 class="card__title">Service Name</h3>
  <p class="card__description">Brief description of the service.</p>
  <a href="/service" class="btn btn--text">Explore More →</a>
</div>
```

### 18.3 Responsive Design Expectations

- **Mobile-first:** Write base styles for mobile, then use `@media (min-width: ...)` to scale up
- **Fluid typography** for hero and large headings:
  ```css
  h1 { font-size: clamp(2rem, 5vw, 3.5rem); }
  ```
- **CSS Grid for card layouts:**
  ```css
  .service-grid {
    display: grid;
    grid-template-columns: repeat(auto-fill, minmax(280px, 1fr));
    gap: var(--space-6);
  }
  ```

### 18.4 Naming Conventions

| Pattern | Convention | Example |
|---|---|---|
| CSS classes | BEM | `card__title`, `btn--primary` |
| CSS custom properties | kebab-case with category prefix | `--color-primary`, `--space-4` |
| Icon files | kebab-case | `arrow-right.svg`, `service-cloud.svg` |
| Image files | kebab-case | `hero-background.webp`, `case-study-ecom.jpg` |
| Component files | PascalCase (if JS/TS) | `ServiceCard.tsx`, `HeroSection.tsx` |

### 18.5 Performance Best Practices

- Load fonts with `font-display: swap` to prevent FOIT
- Preload LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) hero image
- Lazy-load images below the fold with `loading="lazy"`
- Use `will-change: transform` only on actively animated elements (remove after animation)
- Prefer CSS transitions over JavaScript animation for simple hover effects

## 19. Do's and Don'ts

### 19.1 Visual Do's and Don'ts

| Do ✅ | Don't ❌ |
|---|---|
| Use the defined color palette exclusively | Introduce new off-brand colors without approval |
| Maintain clear visual hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3) | Use similar-weight headings stacked together |
| Use white space generously | Cram too much content into one section |
| Alternate section backgrounds (white / gray / navy) | Use the same background for every section |
| Align icons and text consistently | Mix icon sizes inconsistently in the same component |
| Use device mockups with real product screenshots | Use blank or placeholder device frames |

### 19.2 Typography Do's and Don'ts

| Do ✅ | Don't ❌ |
|---|---|
| Use Poppins for headings, Inter for body | Mix 3+ font families on a page |
| Use the defined type scale | Arbitrary font sizes outside the scale |
| Use font weight `600–700` for headings | Set body copy in bold (weight `700`) |
| Use line-height `1.7` for body paragraphs | Use line-height `1.0–1.2` for body text |
| Cap paragraph width at `70ch` | Allow full-width paragraphs on wide screens |

### 19.3 Color Usage Do's and Don'ts

| Do ✅ | Don't ❌ |
|---|---|
| Use `#F5A623` yellow for primary CTAs | Use yellow as a background for large sections |
| Use navy `#0B1F3A` for footer and dark hero sections | Use navy for body text on white |
| Use brand blue for links and interactive elements | Use brand blue for decorative backgrounds |
| Test contrast for all text/background combinations | Assume yellow on white passes contrast |
| Use semantic colors (green=success, red=error) | Use brand blue for error states |

### 19.4 Button & CTA Do's and Don'ts

| Do ✅ | Don't ❌ |
|---|---|
| Use one primary CTA per viewport section | Put two primary (yellow) buttons side by side |
| Write action-oriented CTA text ("Talk to Experts") | Use generic text ("Click Here", "Submit") |
| Match button color to background context | Use yellow button on yellow/warm section background |
| Maintain consistent button padding and border-radius | Mix different border-radius values across buttons |

### 19.5 Content Do's and Don'ts

| Do ✅ | Don't ❌ |
|---|---|
| Lead with outcomes and benefits | Lead with company background and history |
| Use real client names and metrics in case studies | Use vague references ("a large retailer") |
| Write scannable content (headings, bullets, numbers) | Use dense, unbroken paragraphs |
| Use specific calls to action | End pages without a CTA |
| Maintain sentence case for headings | Use ALL CAPS for headings or body text |

## 20. Final Brand Summary

### Who BiztechCS Is

Biztech Consulting & Solutions is a 19-year-old, 200-person technology firm that turns complex digital problems into scalable, measurable solutions. They are not just a dev shop — they are a long-term technology partner for companies that need to move faster, operate smarter, and grow sustainably.

### What the Brand Stands For

**Credibility + Energy.** The brand's navy and blue palette signals enterprise reliability and deep expertise. The yellow accent is the brand's human energy — forward momentum, warmth, and the confidence to say "yes, we can build that."

### How the Visual System Works Together

- **Navy** grounds the brand: authority, stability, trustworthiness
- **Blue** drives action: links, buttons (secondary), interactive affordances
- **Yellow** converts: it is reserved for the single most important CTA on any surface — like a spotlight pointing to what matters most
- **White and light gray** give the design room to breathe
- **Typography** is purposeful: Poppins headings project confidence, Inter body text ensures readability at speed

### How to Use These Guidelines

1. **Designers:** Use the color tokens, type scale, and component specs as the foundation for every mockup. Do not deviate from the token values without a design system proposal.
2. **Developers:** Implement the CSS variables from Section 17 as the first file in every project. Reference tokens — never hard-code hex values.
3. **Content writers:** Re-read the tone guidelines (Section 16) before drafting new pages. Start with the reader's problem, end with a clear action.
4. **Marketing teams:** Every asset — ads, social, email — should use the defined color palette, typography, and CTA language. Off-brand assets dilute the trust this brand has built over 19 years.

### The Brand Promise, Restated

BiztechCS exists to take hard technology problems off the table so their clients can focus on growth. Every design decision — from the confident navy hero to the energetic yellow CTA — should reinforce this single truth:

> **"Tech that works, so you don't have to."**
